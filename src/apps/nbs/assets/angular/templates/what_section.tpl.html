Ship Tip: Package weight and dimensions directly impact your cost.

Shipments are priced either by weight or dimensional weight — a calculation based on package size.

To find dimensional weight, multiply the length, width, and height of your package in inches OR centimeters, rounded up to the nearest whole number. This is your cubic size.

  • For measurements in INCHES, divid cubic size by 139 to find dimensional weight in POUNDS.*
  • For cubic size made in CENTIMETERS, divide by 5,000 for dimensional weight in KILOGRAMS.
  •  

Compare dimensional weight to the actual weight of your package. The larger number determines your shipping charges, so if you have a lightweight item packed in an oversize container, consider using a smaller box if possible.

*NOTE: For shipments within the U.S. and imports to the U.S. from Canada, divide cubic size in inches by 166.

For pallets, length must be greater than or equal to width. Review the Wood Packaging Guidelines to avoid any holds or delays.

Ship Tip: A "reference number" is any combination of numbers and letters you can use to track and identify your shipment.

You can use a purchase order number, customer name, or phrase like "10 crates" or "gift for Mom."

NOTE: To use a reference number as a barcode, use either "14" alphanumeric characters (combination of letters and numbers) or "24" numeric digits (numbers only). Don't use spaces or special characters.

Lithium batteries are high capacity power cells used in electronic devices, tools, and some games and toys. Generally, lithium ion batteries are rechargeable and lithium metal batteries are not.

Ship Tip: Some options for shipping lithium batteries are limited by your shipping route.

Transport to your destination requires some air flight. In certain circumstances, if lithium batteries are not properly packed there is a risk of electrical discharge or overheating due to air pressure changes in flight. Your options for shipping lithium batteries are limited as a result.

More options for shipping regulated items like batteries are available with a UPS dangerous goods contract. You will also need a lithium metal battery shipper pre-approval agreement.
