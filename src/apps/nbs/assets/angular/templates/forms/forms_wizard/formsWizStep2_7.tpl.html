Ship Tip: How do I know if I need an export license?

Only about 5% of exports require an export license. If your shipment involves government or military agencies, certain countries or territories or end user, or if a product has a "dual-use" military or weapons-engineering function, you may need a license or an exception code to export your product.

The Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS) divides licensed products into 10 categories:

Each regulated product has a 5-digit export control classification number (ECCN). If you're exporting a retail item manufactured by another company, chances are they've already classified the item. Look for the ECCNs on the company's website, or find your product on the Commerce Control List: www.bis.doc.gov

Even if your product is listed, it doesn't always mean you need an export license. Every ECCN has one or more "reasons for control," which do not apply equally to all countries or territories. Find your country or territory of export in the Commerce County Chart to see if any "reasons for control" apply: Commerce Country Chart

If you need an export license:

Apply online with BIS through the SNAP-R portal. There's no fee for filing. Be aware, though, that it can take up to six weeks to get a license processed and approved, depending on volume.
